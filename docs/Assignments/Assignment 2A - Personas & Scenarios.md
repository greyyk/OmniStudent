## **Part 1: User Personas** 

## **Persona 1: The Overwhelmed Working Student- Ruby** 

**Personalization: Ruby is a 20 year old college student who works part time while taking a full course load. She is motivated and wants to succeed, but her schedule constantly feels out of control. Ruby uses sticky notes, planners, and apps, but her unpredictable schedule makes it hard to stay consistent. Job-related: Ruby works part-time at a local retail store where her weekly schedule changes frequently. Her manager often adds or shifts hours with little notice, making it difficult for her to plan study time or stay consistent with her academic routine.** 

**Education: Ruby is enrolled full** ‑ **time and takes a challenging mix of courses that require regular studying and preparation. Her academic success is important to her, but her work schedule limits the time she can dedicate to school. Because she often studies late at night or in short bursts, she struggles to keep up with deadlines. Ruby would benefit from tools that help her organize tasks, plan study sessions, and adapt to her constantly changing availability.** 

**Relevance: OmniStudent is highly relevant to Ruby because it directly addresses the** ‑ **challenges she faces as a full time student with an unpredictable work schedule. Ruby needs a tool that can automatically reorganize her study plans when her job hours change, remind her of upcoming deadlines, and help her stay on top of assignments even when she’s exhausted from work. OmniStudent reduces her stress by centralizing her tasks, grades, and schedules in one place, giving her a sense of control she currently lacks. For Ruby, the app isn’t just convenient—it’s essential for maintaining her academic performance while balancing the demands of her job.**

**Relevance: James would use OmniStudent because it would give him a more interactive and student focused system than a normal calendar. ## **Part 2: User Scenarios** 

## **Scenario 1 (for Ruby)** 

**Scenario Name: Solving Scheduling Conflicts and Increasing Academic Performance.** 

**Personas Involved: Ruby** 

**Overall Objective: Ruby is heavily involved with both work and school, her unqualified and incompetent management team at her job repeatedly fails to maintain a static work schedule for her as a part-time employee. The first goal is to create a system that will allow Ruby to track her assignment due dates, assignments, and schedule time during her off hours at work to complete her study sessions.** 

**Problem that can't be addressed by existing systems: Ruby uses sticky notes, planners, and apps separately. These tools can be used to organize information, but they can’t adjust to utilizing her constantly changing schedule. They also do not help manage her time wisely for studying and completing her assignments.** 

**What's involved: Ruby adds her work schedule and her school schedule to OmniStudent. She also enters her current grades, target grades for each class, and assignments. When study times conflict with her unpredictable work schedule, OmniStudent highlights it and recommends study sessions during her downtime.** 

**Possible ways the problem could be tackled: An API could be created  within omnistudent to link the distance learning platform to the students native calendar installed on her devices. If an API link is not an option** 

**OmniStudent could be developed into a mobile application to allow for the student to have direct access on her devices, this would include a notification system to send alerts to went items are due.** 

## **Scenario 2 (for David)** 

**Scenario Name: Balancing Exams, Clubs and Group Projects** 

## **Personas Involved: David** 

**Overall Objective: David wants to stay ahead on his academics while managing club activities and group project deadlines without overcommitting.** 

**Problem that can't be addressed by existing systems: David uses a calendar, notes app, and grade calculator separately. These tools can store information, but they don’t show how all his academic an club activities compete for the same space. They also do not help him decide when to begin studying depending on weight, grade, and upcoming events.** 

**What's involved: David adds his class deadlines, exam dates, project milestones, and volunteer events to OmniStudent. He also enters his current grades as well as his target grades for each class. When conflicts arise between study times and club events, OmniStudent highlights it and recommends an early study session.** 

**Possible ways the problem could be tackled: OmniStudent can provide a priority task board that ranks tasks by due date, grade weight, and time to complete tasks. It could include grade tracking, conflict warnings, and a weekly workload overview. The app could also recommend when David should move nonacademic commitments if his academic workload becomes too much.** 

**Scenario 3 (for Sarah)** 

**Scenario Name: Adapting to a sick kid** 

**Personas Involved: Sarah (Marketing major/Mother/full-time employee) Overall Objective: Keep track of a research assignment due date and find alternative times to complete it when her kid gets sick.** 

**Problem that can't be addressed by existing systems: A static calendar can’t really adjust to live plans. It doesn’t intelligently recommended new times to do assignments based on fluctuating personal events.** 

**What's involved: She wakes her kid up for the morning, and unfortunately her kid isn’t feeling well, this means sarah will miss her planned 1h study session in the morning. Her assistant is due in 2 days, and she needs to figure out when she can finish it all.** 

**Possible ways the problem could be tackled: Sarah would log into OmniStudent and mark out 2 blocks with a personal event / emergency. The system would mark all events during this block as missed, the assignments, and the study session. It then will automatically create new reminders, intelligently scheduling study times during her updated lunch breaks and post-bedtime hours.**
